# Stories vs. Facts

Separating narrative from data. The camera test: would a camera record this, or is it interpretation? The integrity audit: where are your actions out of alignment with your word, and what story are you telling to cover the gap? Confronting uncomfortable facts is brief and actionable; elaborating stories is complex and paralyzing. The goal is accuracy in service of action, not elimination of meaning.

## Quick Reference
- **Best for**: Breaking narrative loops, closing integrity gaps, grounding decisions in data
- **Complexity**: Low (simple concept, difficult honest application)
- **Time to apply**: 15-20 minutes for situation analysis; 30 minutes for integrity audit
- **Pairs with**: Structured Self-Coaching (fact-gathering in Q1), Failure Modes (stories that maintain modes), Psychological Contracts (contracts are stories)
