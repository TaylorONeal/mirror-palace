# Developmental Stages: Safety > Structure > Autonomy

Growth follows a non-negotiable sequence: Safety (felt psychological security), then Structure (clear expectations and scaffolding), then Autonomy (self-direction). You cannot skip stages. Premature challenge harms; prolonged safety stunts. The art is accurately assessing which stage someone is in and providing what that stage requires. Stages are domain-specific -- a person can be in different stages across different areas of life.

## Quick Reference
- **Best for**: Assessing what kind of support is needed and why interventions are or are not landing
- **Complexity**: Medium (three stages, but accurate assessment requires attunement)
- **Time to apply**: 15-20 minutes for domain-level assessment
- **Pairs with**: Structured Self-Coaching (a Structure-stage tool), Failure Modes (stage mismatches), Embodied Awareness (body tracks stage)
