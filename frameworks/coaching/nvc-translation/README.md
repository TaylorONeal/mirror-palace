# NVC Translation

A structured rewriting engine grounded in Marshall Rosenberg's four components — Observation, Feeling, Need, Request (OFNR). Converts reactive, judgmental, or coercive language into mode-aware NVC-aligned expression while refusing to sanitize abuse, evidence, or safety language.

This framework is the **outward expression** companion to [Needs & Feelings Clarity](../needs-feelings-clarity/) (NFC). NFC recovers usable signal from internal experience; NVC Translation produces a new utterance from that signal.

## Quick Reference
- **Best for**: drafting an outgoing message after a conflict, generating an empathy guess, producing clean boundary language, or making the request-vs-demand distinction visible
- **Complexity**: medium
- **Time to apply**: 5–10 minutes per utterance
- **Pairs with**: Needs & Feelings Clarity (do this first), Distortion Detection, Linguistic Reframing, Manipulation Watchouts (boundary mode)

## Modes

1. **Self-expression rewrite** — convert blame/diagnosis/coercion in your own outgoing message into observation, feeling, need, request.
2. **Empathy translation** — render another person's reactive speech as a hedged guess about their feelings and needs.
3. **Boundary rewrite** — produce a clear limit. Less elaboration, more behavioral clarity. Direct imperatives are valid here.
4. **Clarity request** — generate a question that surfaces the missing observation, intent, or need.
5. **Preserve-original** — keep the original wording for evidence/legal/HR/abuse contexts; optionally produce a separate companion NVC formulation.

## What this framework refuses to do

- Sanitize abuse, harassment, or threats into "I feel" sentences that erase reality.
- Soften safety-critical directness.
- Produce a "request" whose hidden function is to punish "no."
- Assert another person's inner state as fact.
- Tone-police clean, appropriate anger.
