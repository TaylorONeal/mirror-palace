# Family Systems Differentiation

A framework for understanding how emotional systems operate in families and relationships. Maps the spectrum from emotional fusion (losing self in relationship) to differentiation (maintaining self while staying connected). Covers triangulation, multigenerational transmission, emotional cutoff, over/under-functioning, and practical differentiation moves.

## Quick Reference
- **Best for**: Understanding why you lose yourself in certain relationships and how to maintain your identity while staying connected
- **Complexity**: High
- **Time to apply**: Initial assessment 45-60 minutes; differentiation is a lifelong practice
- **Pairs with**: Four-F Survival Types, Emotionally Immature Parents, Psychological Contracts
