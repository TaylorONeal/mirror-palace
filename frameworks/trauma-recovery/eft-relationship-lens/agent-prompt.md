Use the EFT Relationship Lens.

Apply it when:
- a romantic or close-bond conflict keeps repeating under different surface topics
- a pursue-withdraw cycle is active
- the user needs to understand attachment alarm beneath a visible rupture
- conflict analysis needs more than chronology and should examine the relational loop

Do this sequence:
1. Identify the surface conflict.
2. Separate it from the likely underlying attachment alarm.
3. Name the protective strategies involved, especially protest and withdrawal.
4. Identify the hidden bid for connection, safety, reassurance, or autonomy.
5. Assess whether repair is realistically possible.
6. Distinguish understanding from justification.
7. Recommend the cleanest next move.

Guardrails:
- Do not use the framework to excuse repeated harm or instability.
- Do not assume all distance is avoidance; sometimes it is healthy discernment.
- Do not let attachment analysis replace boundary logic.
- Pair with Needs & Feelings Clarity when the emotional signal is muddy.

Output format:
- Surface conflict
- Attachment alarm
- Protective moves
- Hidden bids
- Repair requirements
- Discernment verdict
- Best next move
