## Core principle stack

1. Replace apology with gratitude.
2. Replace hedge with statement.
3. Replace blame with experience.
4. Replace why with how.
5. Replace performance with presence.

## 1) Workplace voice (email, Slack, meetings)

### Direct swaps

| Drop | Pick up |
|---|---|
| Sorry for the delay | Thanks for your patience |
| No problem / no worries | Always happy to help |
| Hopefully that makes sense | Let me know if anything's unclear |
| Does that make sense? | Let me know what's unclear |
| I think maybe we should... | It'd be best if we... |
| Just checking in | When you have a moment, can you confirm... |
| I just wanted to ask... | I have one question about... |
| Does that work? | Please confirm if that works |
| Sorry to bother you | When you have a sec... |
| I might be wrong but... | Here's how I see it... |
| I'll try to get it done | I'll have it to you by [time] |
| I don't know | Let me find out and circle back |
| Maybe we could... | Let's... |
| I feel like... | I think... / The data shows... |

### Words to de-emphasize

- just
- sorry (unless actual fault)
- kind of / sort of
- maybe / I think (when claim is already clear)

### Structural guidance

- Specific deadline beats open-ended ask
- Lead with the ask in sentence 1
- Prefer one direct sentence + context

## 2) Working with managers and teams

### Reactive to proactive

| Reactive | Proactive |
|---|---|
| What should I work on this week? | Here are my proposed priorities. Are you aligned? |
| What should we cover tomorrow? | Here are topics for tomorrow's meeting. Anything you'd add? |
| What should I do? | Here are my proposed next steps. What do you think? |
| How do I do XYZ? | Here's what I tried first. I'd love a sanity check. |

### Less defensive replacements

- "That's not what I meant." → "Let me say that differently."
- "Well, actually..." → "Here's a piece you may not have."
- "It wasn't my fault." → "Here's what I'd do differently next time."

### Senior-language upgrades

- "Sorry to push back, but..." → "I see it differently. Here's why."
- "Whenever you get a chance" → "I need this by [date/time]."
- "I'll try to make it work" → "I can do X. I can't do Y."

## 3) Tough conversations

### DIFFICULT mnemonic

- Decide
- Invite
- Focus
- Feelings
- Impact
- Choose curiosity
- Understand
- Look for solution
- Talk again later

### Useful openers

- "Is now a good time?"
- "I want to talk so things get better for both of us."
- "How are you feeling about this?"

### Tone-shift swaps

| From | To |
|---|---|
| They messed up | Here's what I noticed |
| I need to win | Help me see your side |
| Why did this happen? | How can we fix it? |

### De-escalating passive-aggression

- "Help me understand what you meant by that."
- "I want to make sure I'm reading this right. Can you say more?"

## 4) Saying no and boundaries

### Direct no templates

- "No, that doesn't work for me."
- "I'm not able to take this on."
- "I'm going to pass."
- "No, but here's what I can do..."
- "I'm at capacity."

### Gratitude over apology

- "Thanks for your patience" over "Sorry I'm late"
- "Thanks for understanding" over "Sorry for the inconvenience"

## 5) Stop minimizing yourself

Phrases to reduce:
- "It's no big deal"
- "I'm probably overthinking it"
- "I'm being too sensitive"
- "Sorry, this is silly"

Interpretation pattern: many anxious phrases signal reassurance-seeking rather than clear position-taking.

## 6) Anxious vs secure speech

| Anxious | Secure |
|---|---|
| Are you mad at me? | I'd love to know what you're feeling. |
| Did I do something wrong? | Let me know if anything came up for you. |
| Was that okay? | Here's what I'm taking from that. |

Guideline: name feelings as observations/data, not fixed identity.

---

## 7) Self-talk reframes

| Ego voice | Wise voice |
|---|---|
| I shouldn't have done that. | What caused me to react that way? |
| Why did I say something stupid? | I am safe to be heard. |

How-over-why pattern:
- "Why did this happen?" → "How do I want to respond now?"

---

## 8) Listening and ownership

### Owned statements

| Blame | Owned |
|---|---|
| You never take the garbage out! | When you don't take the garbage out, I feel frustrated. |

### Validation upgrade

- Invalidating: "It's not a big deal"
- Validating: "Clearly this is a big deal to you. How can I support you?"

---

## 9) Authentic vs performative connection

- People-pleasing suppresses needs to avoid conflict.
- Authentic kindness includes boundaries + compassionate truth-telling.

Micro-shift:
- "Thanks for making me happy" → "Thanks for supporting my happiness."

---

## 10) Charisma, presence, and vocabulary

### Presence cues

- Slow down
- Hold eye contact one beat longer
- Ask one specific question
- Use intentional pauses

### Replace vague intensifier

When "really" is weak, use precise alternatives (e.g., genuinely, clearly, significantly, deeply).

---

## 11) Interview language

Pattern: answer with specific contribution + evidence, avoid self-deprecating filler.

Example:
- "Why should we hire you?" → "My project management results in [specific area] match this team's goals."

---

## 12) Filtering what to say/do

Decision sequence:
1. Am I obliged?
2. Will it matter?
3. Am I capable?
4. Is there reward?
5. Would I enjoy it?
6. Would anyone else do it?

## Quick reference (overlay extraction)

- Remove: just, kind of, sort of, maybe (when unnecessary), habitual apology
- Add: specific ask, explicit deadline, ownership language
- Conflict frame: "When [behavior], I felt [emotion], and I want [request]."
- Boundary frame: concise no + optional alternative
- Rethink frame: "How do I want to respond?"
