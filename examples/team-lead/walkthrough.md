# Team Lead — Mirror Palace Walkthrough

You lead a small team. You balance your own work with supporting others, navigating organizational dynamics, and making decisions that affect people beyond yourself.

## Recommended Frameworks (Start Here)

1. **Behavioral Signal Reading** — Learn to read the room. Notice when authority is being performed vs genuine, when someone is disengaging, when politics is overriding merit.
2. **Reversibility Classification** — Help your team make faster decisions on two-way doors. Save the careful analysis for one-way doors (hiring, architecture, strategy).
3. **Jobs to Be Done** — Understand what your team members are "hiring" their role for: functional (career growth?), emotional (belonging?), social (status?).
4. **Executive Function Model** — Design your team's processes to support initiation, shift, and planning. Don't assume motivation.
5. **Distortion Detection** — Notice when you or your team are catastrophizing about deadlines, using all-or-nothing thinking about launches, or making should-statements about process.

## Recommended Agent Archetypes

| Archetype | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| The Briefer | Daily synthesis of team status, priorities, and blockers |
| The Watcher | Monitor changes across repos, documents, and communication for drift |
| The Strategist | Track team goals and pipeline, flag stalls |

## Status Domains to Initialize

| Domain | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Career & Work | Your leadership growth, not just team output |
| Community | Your team IS a community. Track belonging and dynamics. |
| Personal Growth | Leadership is a skill. What are you learning? |

## People Records

For each direct report:
- **Support %**: How much are you supporting their growth?
- **Challenge %**: How much are you constructively pushing them?
- **Needs Met**: What do they need from you that they're getting?
- **Needs Unmet**: What are they not getting?

For your manager/stakeholders:
- Same analysis in reverse — what do they need from you?

## Key Influence Defense Applications

As a team lead, people will:
- Use **authority inflation** to bypass your decisions
- Apply **commitment/consistency** to hold you to statements made in different contexts
- Use **social proof** to pressure changes ("everyone else's team does it this way")

Read `manipulation-watchouts` to recognize these patterns.
