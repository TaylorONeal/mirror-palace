# Personal Growth — Mirror Palace Walkthrough

You're using Mirror Palace primarily for self-understanding and personal development. Career, relationships, health, identity, routines. You want a system that helps you notice patterns, make clearer decisions, and act more intentionally.

## Recommended Frameworks (Start Here)

1. **Awareness as Intervention** — The foundation. Start by observing patterns before trying to overhaul them.
2. **Failure Modes** — Identify your top recurring failure modes. Which patterns show up under stress or drift?
3. **Four-F Survival Types** — Understand your stress response. Fight, flight, freeze, or fawn can shape downstream behavior.
4. **Ikigai Diagnostic** — Use it as a gap-locator. Where is the misalignment between love, skill, need, and earning?
5. **Structured Self-Coaching** — Create a regular review practice so insight turns into action.

## Recommended Agent Archetypes

| Archetype | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| The Mirror | The most important one for personal growth. Pattern detection across weeks. Writes reflections that surface what you're not seeing. |
| The Briefer | Daily grounding. Keeps you aware of priorities and domain health. |

## Status Domains — All 10

For personal growth, you can eventually initialize all 10 domains. On day one, though, start with the areas that are most active or most unclear:

- **Money & Finances** — Financial stress is real, not philosophical
- **Career & Work** — What you do and whether it aligns
- **Health & Fitness** — Foundation for everything else
- **Fun & Recreation** — Neglecting this is a pattern worth catching
- **Environment** — Does your physical space support your goals?
- **Community** — Belonging matters more than most people admit
- **Family & Friends** — Inherited patterns live here
- **Partner & Love** — Where attachment and growth intersect
- **Personal Growth** — What you're actively learning and practicing
- **Spirituality** — Whatever this means to you — depth, meaning, practice

## Personality Assessments

Use these selectively:
1. **Big Five** — Useful for broad trait tendencies.
2. **Enneagram** — Useful if motivational patterns resonate for you.
3. **MBTI** — Useful mainly as a language tool, not as hard truth.

The scan skill can help suggest relevant frameworks, but you do not need to turn this into a full assessment project on day one.

## People Records — Key Relationships

For your 5-10 most important relationships:
- Track support% and challenge% — the best relationships are high in both
- List needs met and unmet — this reveals where you're settling and where you're thriving
- Note communication quality — are you being real or editing?

## Decisions Ledger

Your current open life decisions. Even listing them is clarifying:
- Career direction
- Relationship status
- Where to live
- How to spend time
- What to invest in (money, energy, attention)

Apply reversibility-classification to each. You'll likely find most are more reversible than they feel.

## Weekly Practice

1. **Daily**: Run the briefing template (3 min)
2. **Daily**: Check-in protocol before work (1 min)
3. **Weekly**: Full reflection using reflection-template.md (20-30 min)
4. **Weekly**: Review The Mirror's reflection if deployed
5. **Monthly**: Review all domain scores, update stale records, run scan
