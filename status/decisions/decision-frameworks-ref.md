# Decision Framework Quick Reference

When facing a decision, use this mapping to determine which frameworks to apply. Start with the first applicable question and work down.

## Primary Router

| Question | If Yes | Framework | Path |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|
| Is this decision reversible? | Classify first | `reversibility-classification` | `frameworks/decision-making/reversibility-classification/` |
| Is this a two-way door? | Decide quickly, don't over-analyze | `reversibility-classification` | Stop here — just decide |
| Is this high-stakes / life-changing? | Apply regret test | `regret-minimization` | `frameworks/decision-making/regret-minimization/` |
| Does this involve career or purpose? | Check alignment | `ikigai-diagnostic` | `frameworks/decision-making/ikigai-diagnostic/` |
| Are there competing options with no clear winner? | Test against north star | `north-star-test` | `frameworks/decision-making/north-star-test/` |
| Am I emotionally charged about this? | Check for distortions | `distortion-detection` | `frameworks/cognitive-therapy/distortion-detection/` |
| Am I telling myself a story about this? | Separate story from facts | `stories-vs-facts` | `frameworks/coaching/stories-vs-facts/` |
| Does this involve other people? | Check their records | People records | `status/people/` |
| Are there invisible agreements at play? | Surface the contracts | `psychological-contracts` | `frameworks/pattern-detection/psychological-contracts/` |
| Does this feel urgent but might not be? | Check for manipulation | `manipulation-watchouts` | `frameworks/influence-defense/manipulation-watchouts/` |
| Am I avoiding this decision? | Check what's underneath | `failure-modes` | `frameworks/pattern-detection/failure-modes/` |
| Will I be thinking about this in 10 years? | Full analysis warranted | Apply all relevant frameworks | — |

## Decision Type Matrix

| Decision Type | Example | Reversibility | Frameworks to Apply |
|---------------|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| Tool/process change | New app, new routine | Two-way | `reversibility-classification` only — just try it |
| Side project start | Weekend experiment | Two-way | `north-star-test` (is it aligned?), then go |
| Job application | Applying somewhere | Two-way | `ikigai-diagnostic`, `north-star-test` |
| Job acceptance | Taking an offer | One-way-ish | `regret-minimization`, `ikigai-diagnostic`, `north-star-test` |
| Job resignation | Leaving current role | One-way | Full analysis: `regret-minimization`, `ikigai-diagnostic`, `reversibility-classification`, check `FIN` domain |
| Relationship escalation | Moving in, commitment | One-way-ish | `regret-minimization`, `psychological-contracts`, check people records |
| Relationship ending | Breaking up, cutting off | One-way | `regret-minimization`, `stories-vs-facts`, `distortion-detection` |
| Financial commitment | Lease, loan, investment | One-way | `reversibility-classification`, `regret-minimization`, check `FIN` domain |
| Geographic move | New city, new country | One-way | Full analysis: all decision frameworks + `ENV`, `COM`, `FAM` domains |
| Health change | Surgery, major diet shift | Varies | `reversibility-classification`, `regret-minimization`, check `HEA` domain |
| Skill investment | Course, certification | Two-way | `north-star-test`, `ikigai-diagnostic`, check `PGR` domain |
| Boundary setting | Saying no, limiting access | Two-way-ish | `psychological-contracts`, `family-systems-differentiation`, check people records |

## Speed Rules

1. **Two-way doors**: Decide in under 24 hours. Document briefly. Don't agonize.
2. **One-way doors with low stakes**: Decide in under a week. Apply 1-2 frameworks.
3. **One-way doors with high stakes**: Take the time needed. Apply 3+ frameworks. Do the regret check. Consult people records. Sleep on it at least once.
4. **If a decision has been pending for 30+ days**: It's either not important enough to decide (downgrade) or you're avoiding it (apply `failure-modes`).

## Red Flags in Decision-Making

Watch for these patterns (agents should flag them):
- Agonizing over two-way doors (perfectionism, fear of commitment)
- Rushing one-way doors (impulsivity, discomfort with uncertainty)
- Deciding based on "should" (check `distortion-detection` for should-statements)
- Deciding to avoid discomfort rather than toward a goal (check `four-f-survival-types`)
- Asking everyone's opinion instead of deciding (check `family-systems-differentiation` for differentiation issues)
- Making the same type of bad decision repeatedly (check `failure-modes` and `closed-loop-learning`)
