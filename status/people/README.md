# People Tracking System

People records capture the relational landscape of your life. Every significant person gets a file. The system is designed to surface patterns in how you relate to others and how others affect your trajectory.

## Key Metrics

### Support % (0-100)
How much this person supports your growth. High support means they encourage your goals, validate your direction, show up when it matters, and make you feel capable of more. This is not about whether they agree with everything you do — it's about whether their presence in your life makes you more likely to become who you want to be.

- **80-100**: Actively champions your growth. Celebrates wins. Shows up in hard times.
- **50-79**: Generally supportive but inconsistent, distracted, or limited in capacity.
- **20-49**: Neutral or mildly undermining. May support verbally but not in action.
- **0-19**: Actively discouraging, dismissive, or sabotaging of your growth.

### Challenge % (0-100)
How much this person constructively challenges you. High challenge means they push you to think harder, call out your blind spots, hold you to your own standards, and don't let you coast. This is not about conflict — it's about the kind of friction that makes you sharper.

- **80-100**: Regularly asks hard questions. Holds you accountable. Won't let you hide.
- **50-79**: Occasionally challenges, but tends to go along or avoid confrontation.
- **20-49**: Rarely pushes back. Agreeable to a fault. You can't grow from their feedback.
- **0-19**: Never challenges. Pure validation or pure avoidance.

### The Best Relationships
The healthiest relationships score high on BOTH metrics. A person who supports you (80+) AND challenges you (80+) is rare and valuable. This combination means they want the best for you AND they're willing to push you toward it.

**Common patterns to watch:**
- **High Support, Low Challenge** — Comfortable but stagnant. You won't grow from this person's feedback.
- **Low Support, High Challenge** — Critic without care. Their challenges feel like attacks, not growth.
- **Low Support, Low Challenge** — Dead weight. The relationship exists out of habit or obligation.
- **High Support, High Challenge** — The gold standard. Protect these relationships.

### Communication Quality (0-100)
How honest, direct, and emotionally safe the conversations are. Can you say what you actually think? Can they?

## Creating a Person Record

1. Copy `_template.md` to a new file named with a lowercase, hyphenated identifier (e.g., `college-roommate.md`, `work-mentor.md`)
2. Fill in the overview table
3. List needs met and unmet
4. Link to relevant domain issues and decisions
5. Note relevant frameworks (e.g., `psychological-contracts` for invisible agreements, `family-systems-differentiation` for family members)

## How Agents Use People Records

- **The Mirror** reads people records to detect relational patterns over time
- **The Tracker** compares stated relationship goals to actual contact frequency
- **The Strategist** checks people links when evaluating decisions that involve others
- **The Scan skill** can populate people records from conversation analysis and connected services

## Updating People Records

People data should update whenever:
- A significant interaction happens (update Last Contact, Notes)
- A pattern is observed by an agent (update Patterns & Notes)
- A domain issue changes that involves this person (update Linked Issues)
- A decision is made that affects the relationship (update Linked Decisions)
- Your assessment of Support/Challenge/Communication quality shifts (update scores with reasoning in Notes)

## Privacy

People records contain subjective assessments. They are for personal reflection, not for sharing with the people described. Keep them in private repos only.
